History of Vatican City

Founding of Vatican City

Vatican city was founded on February 11, 1929, after Mussolini decided he would rather not risk a Religious civil war.

The Government of Vatican City

Vatican city historically is lead by the King of Vatican City also known as the pope. It is the one of the only Elected absolute Monarchy in the world. The population of Vatican city is not permanent and are only citizens as long as they work there.

The History of Vatican

The Vatican’s history as the seat of the Catholic Church began with the construction of a basilica over St. Peter’s grave in Rome in the 4th century A.D. St. Peter’s Basilica became a spiritual center for Christian pilgrims, leading to the development of housing for clergymen and the formation of a marketplace that became the thriving commercial district of Borgo.
